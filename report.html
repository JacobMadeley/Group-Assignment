PMI

your group should spend some time reflecting on the process and describe here the Pluses, Minuses and Interesting aspects of your teamwork process and result

Pluses: Easy communication, process of doing pages was easy (pick a page you want to do). An aspect we liked is how flexible we were able to get the divs to display people on the Artists page and Join Us pages, no tables were used (divs were coded to be like tables, but no "tables" used) and you just code up however many people per line you want as you need to. Another aspect we liked was how flexible the design was overall, considering no bootstrap or viewport considerations were put in place, the website scaled fairly well to mobile size and smaller window sizes.

Minuses: Just picking pages led to similar but also generally different designs, not being able to use the same IDE (Jacob-Dreamweaver, Nicholas-PHP Storm) also caused troubles when opening up the code, as the IDE's format the code differently, making it hard to work on each others work to fix or add parts to the website. Not doing the feeder page is also a huge negative. Code is also not commented and all of the images, inparticular on the Artists page does not have ALT text for screen readers or incase the images don't load(didn't get time)

Interesting: (including significant lessons) How the div "tables" were so fluid. Much of the site is the same code, just repeated with different links, images and text; overall the website does not have too much different code (however, it does take a long time to copy paste what you have made, then change the small parts that make the site work). Much of the CSS could most likely be improved, but as we ran out of time, we did not clean it up.

The most significant lesson from this project; do not leave the project until week 13!, we did milestone 1, got caught up in other assignments and then we had to hand in this website, leaving too much work to do, especially with one of us having a few more life commitments than the other, meaning the website is far worse off than it could have potentially been (only our own fault thought).

Something else important is understanding the content, even after completing what we are hading in, we are sure that we have missed many parts of what we needed to do, the feeder page being one of these. Overall we were unsure of what content to use on most pages and essentially recreated the old website with some better aesthetics.

Overall, we were unhappy with what we have produced as we know we could have done better, and should have worked harder to accomplish what we set out to do.
